Screening threshold

Screening sits below the verification match bar (85) on purpose: a missed true match is worse than a reviewed false one, so the bar to surface a hit is lower and a human dispositions what surfaces.

Privacy & sources

The name you screen never leaves your browser. It is matched here, client-side, against lists already loaded on the page — there is no backend, no screening API, no analytics. Hosting the tool publishes the tool, never your query.

This public page ships a synthetic sample list. A local build can load real published lists (OFAC, UN, EU sanctions; OpenSanctions for PEPs; FATF/EU country risk) — each attributed in SOURCES.md. OpenSanctions is CC-BY-NC: fine for personal, non-commercial use; commercial use needs their licence.

A secondary aid, not a validated system of record. Real screening decisions belong in your firm's approved, governed system.
